# Family Personas

User personas used for feature validation and focus group discussions. These represent the diversity of households that Famick Home Management serves.

## Sarah — Young Family, Budget-Conscious

- **Household**: Mom of 3 kids (ages 2, 5, and 7). Stay-at-home parent.
- **Cooking**: Cooks almost every meal. Deals with picky eaters and food allergies (nut allergy for the 5-year-old).
- **Budget**: Budget-conscious, tracks grocery spending carefully.
- **Devices**: Uses her phone primarily. Plans on the couch Sunday nights.
- **Planning style**: Plans the full week on Sunday. Only plans dinners — breakfasts and lunches are on autopilot.
- **Pain point**: "I spend 30 minutes every Sunday just figuring out what to cook this week."
- **Key needs**: Quick planning workflow, allergen warnings, kid-friendly meal indicators, low setup barrier, shopping list generation.

## Marcus — Dual-Income, Nutrition-Focused

- **Household**: Dad in a dual-income household, 2 kids (ages 9 and 12). He and his wife split cooking duties.
- **Cooking**: Weekend meal prep. Cares about nutrition and portion control. Tracks macros.
- **Budget**: Moderate — shops at multiple stores, concerned about food waste.
- **Devices**: Uses both web and mobile.
- **Planning style**: Plans dinners for the week and meal-preps lunches on Sundays. Wants the system to surface expiring items and sale items during planning.
- **Pain point**: "We waste so much food because we buy things we don't end up using."
- **Key needs**: Accurate nutrition tracking, portion/serving scaling, stock-aware suggestions, multi-store shopping lists, recipe import from URLs.

## Diana — Single Mom, Teens with Different Diets

- **Household**: Single mom, 2 kids (ages 15 and 17, high school). Works full time.
- **Cooking**: Needs fast, practical meals. The teens are starting to cook for themselves. The 17-year-old is vegetarian.
- **Budget**: Moderate — efficiency matters more than cost tracking.
- **Devices**: Phone primarily, web occasionally.
- **Planning style**: Plans only 2-3 days ahead, maximum. Cannot commit to a full week due to unpredictable teen schedules.
- **Pain point**: "My kids eat at different times and have totally different dietary needs."
- **Key needs**: Per-person meal variants (same base meal, swap protein), flexible partial-week planning, teen engagement features (meal requests/voting), empty slots shouldn't feel like failure.

## Tom & Linda — Fluctuating Household, Elaborate Cook

- **Household**: Couple with 3 kids (ages 16, 19 in college nearby, and 21 in college 2 hours away). The 19-year-old comes home on weekends. Household size fluctuates between 3 and 6 on any given night.
- **Cooking**: Tom is the primary cook. Loves elaborate weekend meals (multi-component dinners, holiday feasts). Linda handles weeknight basics.
- **Budget**: Comfortable — quality and variety over cost.
- **Devices**: Plans on iPad at the kitchen table. Quick edits on phone during the day.
- **Planning style**: Plans the full week but adjusts constantly as household size changes. Needs to quickly swap meals mid-week.
- **Pain point**: "I never know how many people I'm cooking for on any given night."
- **Key needs**: Serving scaling, quick mid-week edits on mobile, multi-component meal support, template weeks (seasonal), drag-and-drop on tablet.

## Kenji — Blended Family, Shared Cooking

- **Household**: Dad of 4 kids (ages 6, 8, 14, and 18 heading to college in fall). Blended family. He and his partner alternate cooking weeks.
- **Cooking**: Alternates weeks with partner. The 14-year-old is learning to cook. They share a grocery budget.
- **Budget**: Shared budget between two adults — coordination is critical.
- **Devices**: Both web and mobile. Partner also actively uses the app.
- **Planning style**: Rotates through ~20-25 meals. Whoever is cooking that week does the planning. Needs shared visibility and no duplicate grocery purchases.
- **Pain point**: "My partner and I keep buying the same things because we don't coordinate."
- **Key needs**: Shared meal plan visibility, concurrent editing without conflicts, cooking assignment per day, favorites/recent meal reuse, push notifications ("tonight's dinner is X, defrost the salmon"), teen cooking engagement.

## Using These Personas

When designing or validating features:

1. **Walk through each persona's primary scenario** — Does the feature work for Sarah planning on her phone Sunday night? Does it work for Diana planning only 2 days ahead?
2. **Check for pain point coverage** — Does the feature directly address at least one persona's stated pain point?
3. **Test edge cases from real households** — Fluctuating headcount (Tom), parallel dietary needs (Diana), shared cooking responsibility (Kenji), food allergies (Sarah, Kenji).
4. **Validate across device contexts** — Phone-first (Sarah, Diana), tablet planning (Tom), dual web+mobile (Marcus, Kenji).
5. **Consider adoption barriers** — Would Sarah use this before her recipe library is built? Would Diana stick with it if empty slots feel like failure?
